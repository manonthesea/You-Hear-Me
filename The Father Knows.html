The Father Knows

1At the child’s birth, no word is spoken of the sorrow
2the father knows to come.

3Worldly existence upon a wobbly axis stares
4up at a carouseling ceiling — 
5iniquities passed to servant hands,
6still warm from the womb and already won.

7Paradox is the eyes with which we are born
8and the means to our peace.

9Joyously, an attendant plants a lily
10eager for spring bloom — 
11one hand in the dirt,
12plump-worm-filled and carcass-covering.

13The hope for salvation is a bulb sunk into decay
14where every child born is a potential savior.
12.24.18
