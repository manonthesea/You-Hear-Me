The Bard Speaks

1 The Bard speaks for a moment past.
2 His song and dance is a quick step, shuffle, and skip,
3 accent and emphasis added for subtle effect,
4 but that is then and this is now,
5 that rhapsody of spring for which I do not sing.
6 A scattered thought and hollow feel are the best I scratch,
7 but the words we share like pen on paper carry significance 
8 that the parsing machines will never catch.

9 In my awkward caesura and empty sayings,
10 a refreshing winter-gust
 of wind smacks
11 of the nuanced understanding when stepping out,
12 into the dark, and onto the front porch under backlit night,
13 where tingling pores of uncovered skin feel a spirit near;
14 there, in that space -- translucent breath 
15 unseen in any real, lasting, photogenic sense -- 
16 is life beyond measurement.

17 In his rambling coherence and my over-complicated nonsense,
18 poetry encodes the human ethos shared by lauded troubadour
19 and your vacuous anxiety-ridden acquaintance.
20 In the unsettled rhythms and rhymes of life,
21 far from the warm summer of our conception,
22 we find ourselves standing in the cold
23 yet alive with word.

10.14.20
