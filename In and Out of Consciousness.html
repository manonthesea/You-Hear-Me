In and Out of Consciousness

1 Almonds and chocolate . . .
2 warm bed on a cold night . . . 
3 souls drifting
4 into a nether . . . 

5 We are here now, and soon somewhere else . . . 

6 Criss-crossing down to the corner store,
7 heels clicking,
8 touch of a plastic world
9 demonstrates that reality exists
10 outside of physicality -- 
11 where locomotive meaning is 
12 couched metaphysically . . . 

13 Sink in . . . 

14 Slack jawed with a head let fall back
15 into that dream
16 that cushions the actuality
17 that nothing is as it seems
18 in front of a screen.

19 When perception of the ephemeral moment 
20 goes a different direction than expected,
21 let the self be alone
22 as it sings to itself . . . 

23 As if my eyes are open . . .

24 I see that the willows know 
25 the possums hide up under the porch 
26 roaming out in the electric lamp light,
27 yet they sway tight-lipped, down by the waterfront,
28 perfectly happy being silent when the world needs to be still.

29 In the day, we know the willows use the breeze to talk to each other, 
30 but as then is now, I watch in reverence.
31 The tracks going up the stairs carry meaning,
32 for heavy feet carry a weight not measured in pounds . . . 

33 I lift a heavy foot and feel your warm feet under the covers . . . 

34 One more waking breath
35 before succumbing to sleep in the next . . .
