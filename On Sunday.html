On Sunday

1 Church people meet around the country,
2 in the same dank, drab basements 
3 and little musty rooms of modesty.
4 The modest keep quiet a righteous idea
5 about a man who spoke up in defense of the poor, 
6 cried in front of his witnesses, and went to the cross, 
7 even though he could have been Barabbas 
8 (without stakes driven through his rope-burned wrists).

9 He would not have wept, been spat upon,

11 sobbing out to unseen father as he suffocates, 
12 is stabbed, and dies in front of his mother.

13 Hope walks into the room looking up 
14 through a turned down brow
15 and sees honesty raised on a modest cross.
16 The righteous idea of love thy enemy hangs 
17 on crudely forged iron nails sledged through hands,  
18 holding the visceral weight of man pulled chest under foot; 
19 carried away, washed by servant hands of no living word, 
20 in resistance to the gravity of death, Christ rises --
21 having chosen death to live through the Word 
22 that love, stewardship, and service reign.

23 Upon aluminum chairs in modest rooms,
24 a humbling spirit presides;
25 looking each other in the eye honestly,
26 the modest wonder if they too are godforsaken.
