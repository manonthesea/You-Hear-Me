Prey to the Weather

1 Probability rises,
2 and happenstance closes in like clouds.
3 Summer’s slow in coming,
4 and time becomes something foreign
5 for a cold-while longer:

6 Empty boardwalks,
7 abandoned homes,
8 and everybody suffocating
9 in their own matter
10 as snow ceaselessly falls.

11 Down in rag-and-bone alley
12 hear the patter of running feet
13 like the fleeting rabbit
14 that’s the clock hung
15 on the wall of a foreclosing home.
