You Through the Eyes of a Buddha

1 Some days when you stop at the swim spot,
2 instead of walking up to the bridge
3 cause you know hot days lie ahead when the river will be low,
4 you swim now while you can.

5 The current is strong enough to wash away the past
6 in which you are told only what you are wanted to remember,
7 so you lay your head back to submerge your ears into the Earth
8 and hear the years as they break off heading down river.

9 Like fingers gripping the riverbed,
10 water and time create nostalgia,
11 but listening long enough to the fluid passing of time, 
12 you hear nature telling you to change.

13 Today the stream moves fast, 
14 yet you slow summertime enough to enjoy the cool present.
15 Rising from the water, like the river
16 you are made anew, at once the same but entirely different.
