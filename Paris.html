Paris

1 During the first days at the turn of another century, 
2 Paris is infinitely young, and time appears linear,
3 forever smelling of hashish.

4 Day becomes dream and dream superfluous.
5 In streets, in stores, among flowers,
6 Paris is inside open-windowed shops.

7 Time passes, and a fading recollection sits upon Butte Chaumont's high-end hill.
8 Paris stares at Sacre-Coeur on full display in front of a setting sun
9 like a spring lover posing nude in front of a dim light.

10 At the end of days, the smell of hashish still hangs on the damp evening air of Paris 
11 as an apparition lingers from the turn of the previous century, 
12 while Paris is on the precipice of another summer.
