Paris

1During the first days at the turn of another century, 
2Paris is infinitely young, and time appears linear,
3forever smelling of hashish.

4Day becomes dream and dream superfluous.
5In streets, in stores, among flowers,
6Paris is inside open-windowed shops.

7Time passes as a fading recollection sits upon Butte Chaumont's high-end hill.
8Paris stares at Sacre-Coeur on full display in front of a setting sun
9like a spring lover posing nude in front of a dim light.

10At the end of days, the smell of hashish still hangs on the damp evening air of Paris 
11as an apparition lingers from the turn of the previous century, 
12while Paris is on the precipice of another summer.
