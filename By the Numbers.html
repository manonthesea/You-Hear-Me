By the Numbers

1 The first day of the second month during the second year of liberation, 
2 already the accounting of Numbers starts with conscripting men. 
3 Men made to fall in line and come forward. 
4 When their names are called, they stand for what?
5 Say nothing of the radicals made among them that day
6 choosing to change their name, kneel, and raise their fist in defiance. 

7 The record of yesteryear and internet news tomorrow 
8 says only one thing of those protesting slaughter for hire:
9 they are delivered, nonetheless, into death -- 
10 rounded up or pushed into the pageus borderlands, always made to fight.
11 Intimidated, coerced not to speak, and put to labor when they refuse, 
12 again and again, the men of the polis run them down with dogs,
13 spraying them with fire hoses and forcibly shaving their heads.

14 No matter the victor, the women cry out 
15 to their God, the God of Love, the God who will give 
16 his only son as debt paid for every sin of every father.
17 Out of sight of the hands counting heads today and coins later this month,
18 made to drink bitter wine, hidden in cellars, 
19 voiceless in the history of God’s bloody will blinded to women fleeing with babes in tears – 
20 Black Madonnas will speak a grand final soliloquy – a new accounting
21 of those accused of sedition against God’s word   
22 and of the sacrificed sons they cannot save.
