Desert Morning Chill

1A swarm of moths freeze to stone, 
2surrounding the forgotten outside lamp.

3From the cold, long after warm-night fete, 
4warm-blooded creatures retire

5to a metabolic state 
6where time exists as it does in utero:

7eyes closed yet fully birthed, 
8given over to the contractions of days, months, and years, 
9carrying our bodies in continuum.

. . .

10The moths exist in the abstract as a human held 
11in their mother’s womb -- 

12neither wholly separate from that cold stone
13that welcomes in the desert morning chill 

14nor wholly together in a union
15that reduces distinct bodies into a singularity.
2.16.19
