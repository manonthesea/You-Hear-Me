You and I

1 A professor from Princeton says aloud, 
2 isolated from but a few out-of-sight
3 galaxies, our world ending will be found.
4 All the stars will burn out blue and bright white. 
5 Black holes will consume every last atom,
6 and foreseeing a “desolate end” come,
7 our future history is hard to fathom;
8 yet when that last tide sucks out through dead sun sunk
9 into a pale, dark sky -- what an end -- 
10 glorious if humans stand to witness.
11 We will be there, you and I, in our children, 
12 to feel the last touch of earth-green existence,
13 praising Love fearlessly for a life lived 
14 with a heart beating and a soul to be saved.

5.2.17
