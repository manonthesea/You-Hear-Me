Eat to Remember

1 After a late night,
2 sometime after death,
3 in the early, purple morning,
4 a friend makes breakfast over an open fire 
5 and repeatedly asks if you love them.

6 Having come to find us even after death, 
7 words over breakfast remind us
8 to tend to the sheep with love.
9 Break bread, partake of the cup, 
10 and remember Love is greatest of these.

11 Love is a meal prepared
12 for the people carrying a heavy yoke.
13 Unexplainable mysteries are no sustenance
14 like a savior serving breakfast at dusk.
15 Eat to remember.
4.29.21
