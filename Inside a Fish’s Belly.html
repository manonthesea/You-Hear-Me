Inside a Fish’s Belly

1We slept outside on the open ground one summer last century. 2After a day advertised with fish jumping into our boats, 3no trout were taking any bait. 4At almost a mile high, 5the sun’s heat pushed up just below the century mark. 6I remember my white thighs burning red 7atop that tin boat just staring at that glass water. 8I suspect it was the first day the planet started warming for good. 9Crane Prairie Reservoir ties time and memory together 10like an improved clinch knot: 11that season is a line threaded through the eye of my mind’s hook. 12As of late, whenever the sun is high and the air is thin, 13the burning white of my high thigh pulls on the line. 14The white peaks years later appear in my memories 15like I’m harvesting dragonfly nymphs today from beneath logs 16along the nearly algae-free water of the shoreline. 17Like a swallowed hook inside a fish’s belly, thinking of time past, 18what is remembered hides a point. 19A nose under the stars, upon anthills, 20and preyed upon by an infinite multitude of mosquitoes, 21half-drunken fathers and their sons sleep, 22only after taking whatever was in Dad’s pocket. 23As much as it took to fall asleep lakeside on that mountain, 24the more it takes it to turn back time and experience it again. 10.6.18
