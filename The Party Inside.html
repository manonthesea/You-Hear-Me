The Party Inside

1 Examining the metaphysics of time and place,
2 concerning ourselves with no more than the wall,
3 the party starts to take a feverish pace
4 with everyone in a satisfactory state,
5 seemingly ready to take part in the festivities of relativity,
6 inhaling the red rose that fills the room of the man
7 saying c’est ne pas une pipe but is still smoking.

8 Soon, the party wants nothing to do with psychoanalytic theory,
9 cursing the red rose for being obtuse and big enough to fill the entire room,
10 so Freud’s out the door and so is the painter.
11 (Great minds think alike. I see no flower in the room,
12 only the girl that gives off that blooming smell,
13 sitting on the couch and holding the stem of her glass
14 as though the whole house should know her scent.)

15 In the early morning hours, the party starts coming out,
16 leading the neighbors to think we are all going mad.
17 By now, the party chases itself about the lawn; 
18 given over to the id, the party is in a splendid fit.
19 Welcome to the 21st century where even egos run amuck.

Circa 2008
