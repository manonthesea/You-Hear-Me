Paegus Trip

1 Dry fish flake on the riverbed.
2 Summer’s long gone, and still selfish sky
3 refuses to give a-plenty if anything.

4 Crossing the creviced bedrock,
5 quiet, dark shadows creep across 
6 the shallows that reap many.

7 Up the bank on the other side,
8 watching the followed back of a body,
9 an internal monologue drifts 

10 listening to mouths creaking
11 in alien tongue, less language 
12 than sounds with meaning.

13 Cannot help but think the Capitol’s racket 
14 fixes vulgar disposition to wit: ransom paid to the right 
15 when one means to say r(e)ich, 

16 before stilled thought wonders,
17 should we keep walking with turned backs
18 or turn back and return to the polis?
