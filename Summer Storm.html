Summer Storm

1 100 plus degree heat is loud
2 when two fronts meet in Texas.
3 The rain falls in sheets,
4 while I’m sure the neighbor’s 
5 newborn sleeps.
6 An electric crack sounds
7 against the pillars of the covered porch.

8 The storm starts, and we fear it 
9 from a distance; we revere it 
10 for a time, but soon women and children disappear inside.
11 Around the house to the garage, I move alone 
12 to hear the atmosphere preach, 
13 while water upon the street pounds
14 and an already wild cat meows loudly out-of-sorts.

15 An I-wouldn’t-call-it-cool breeze
16 lofts a welcomed change and an alive feel.
17 My body reels with energy.
18 Hairs stand charged, and the pores on my arms open.
19 Lightning is close at hand, above the trees,
20 upon the thunder, amongst the clouds,
21 and ready to gnarl and contort.

22 The moment rumbles clear, 
23 unquestionable and sincere,
24 calling witness to the trees stuck here
25 steadfastly weathering the storm. 
26 Synapses snap-fire between my ears 
27 like lightning bolts from as far as the quantum profound
28 bottle-caught through rhyme and metaphor.

8.20.18
