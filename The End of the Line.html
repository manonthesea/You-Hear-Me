The End of the Line

1 Just after the solstice, time seems to slip — 
2 like water over rock — a regular, modern blip, 
3 a stream that buffers and skips.

4 Clouds forever long formed 
5 mount into a generational storm 
6 upon a coastline ill-prepared to see morn.

7 With the sun no longer at its highest
8 and the steady rising rivers about to crest,
9 together let us work on what comes next.

10 Otherwise, time’s opposition to infinity 
11 and tomorrow’s tendency to become history
12 insist our extinction likely.

6.27.19
