Shooting Marbles

1 My father is Peter Pan on acid,
2 the youngest of the Merry Pranksters, 
3 tagging along the return caravan west to California, 
4 making detours, spending nights on communes,
5 drinking mescaline soup,
6 sitting with “turn on, tune in, and drop out” gurus.

7 My father is my father, 
8 and despite never growing older,
9 time still passes in Neverland.
10 History is still made, 
11 sins are still had, 
12 and his are now mine.

13 But perhaps I could be comfortable 
14 in his Pan shoes 
15 even when he wakes,
16 kicks his toe, and screams
17 off to the cold work waiting for him
18 day in and day out;
19 by the time you see him again
20 warmed by a sixer
21 and the ferry dust of his delusions,
22 he is now all grins.

23 Perhaps I can be the best of this boy-man,
24 and wear a smile for the lost boys, 
25 stuck in disbelief
26 that time ever existed 
27 before now
28 when all that matters
29 is shooting marbles.
