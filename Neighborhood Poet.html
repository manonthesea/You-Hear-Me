Neighborhood Poet

1I write poems, written for my loves -- 
2to be read in new-year winter, southern spring, hot summer, and quick fall
3and to be read by those who humor me to speak with synecdoche
4because they know the burning flame-red, soft hair trying my sanity.

5I am a neighborhood poet, who lives and pains 
6the same insufferable cul-de-sac experience 
7as the housewife in her brick ranch down the block 
8clinging to her presupposed, dull belief in a paradox.

9Take these poems; let the word liberate us after the children fall asleep.
10In that nether that plants an unspoken thought within an empty space,
11like time’s warm hand upon a woman’s womb,
12light shines into darkness turning nothingness into possibility.
1.10.18
